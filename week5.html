WebDevelopment Design Process


The website design process from a game designer's perspective with regards to the UX Structural Design Template function and production: I. Defining the Design Goals A. Understanding the game's brand and target audience: The website design should align with the game's brand and appeal to its target audience. B. Defining the website's purpose: The website's purpose should be clear and aligned with the game's goals, such as promoting the game, providing information, or selling merchandise. II. Creating the UX Structural Design Template A. Planning the website structure: Plan out the website's structure, including the main sections, sub-sections, and hierarchy of information. B. Creating wireframes: Create wireframes to visually represent the website's layout and content, including the placement of navigation, images, and text. C. Developing the UX structural design template: Develop the UX structural design template based on the wireframes, including the use of color, typography, and other visual elements. III. Functionality and Production A. Developing website functionality: Develop website functionality, such as forms, buttons, and interactive elements, to provide a seamless user experience. B. Producing visual assets: Produce visual assets, such as images and videos, to enhance the website's design and appeal to the target audience. C. Testing and refining: Test the website for functionality and usability, and refine the design based on user feedback and testing results. D. Launching and maintaining: Launch the website and maintain it over time, including updating content, fixing bugs, and adding new features.
